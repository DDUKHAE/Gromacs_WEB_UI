                  Box Type
                  

                    ?
                    Determines periodic boundary box geometry. Dodecahedron is highly recommended to minimize box volume and computing resources.
                  

                

                

                  
                

              


              
              

                

                  Edge Distance
                  

                    ?
                    Specifies the minimum distance (nm) between the solute outer boundary and the simulation box walls to prevent self-interaction.
                  

                

                

                  
                

              

            

          


          
          

            

              
              

                

                  Salt Type
                  

                    ?
                    The type of salt ions to be added for simulating physiological ionic strength.
                  

                

                

                  
                

              


              
              

                

                  Concentration
                  

                    ?
                    The concentration of salt in molarity (M). Standard physiological ionic strength is 0.15M.
                  

                

                

                  
                

              


              
              

                

                  Neutralize
                  

                    ?
                    Automatically adds counter-ions (cations/anions) to neutralize the net charge of the molecular system.
                  

                

                

                  
                

              

            

          


          
          

            

              
              
              

                

                  

                    Ensemble & Thermodynamics
                    

                      ?
                      Configures the simulation ensemble (temperature, pressure, time) and coupling algorithms.
                    

                  

                  ▼
                

                

                  

                    
                    

                      

                        Temperature
                        

                          ?
                          The target temperature in Kelvin (K) for the temperature coupling. Room temperature is typically 300K.
                        

                      

                      

                        
                      

                    


                    
                    

                      

                        Pressure
                        

                          ?
                          The target pressure in bar (1.0 bar ≈ 1.0 atm) for the pressure coupling.
                        

                      

                      

                        
                      

                    


                    
                    

                      

                        Sim Time
                        

                          ?
                          The total physical time in nanoseconds (ns) for the molecular dynamics production run.
                        

                      

                      

                        
                      

                    


                    
                    

                      

                        Thermostat
                        

                          ?
                          The temperature coupling algorithm (thermostat) to maintain constant temperature. V-rescale is recommended.
                        

                      

                      

                        
                      

                    


                    
                    

                      

                        Barostat
                        

                          ?
                          The pressure coupling algorithm (barostat) to maintain constant pressure. Parrinello-Rahman is recommended.
                        

                      

                      

                        
                      

                    

                  

                

              


              
              

                

                  

                    Performance & Output
                    

                      ?
                      Configures integration time step and coordinate/trajectory output frequencies.
                    

                  

                  ▼
                

                

                  

                    
                    

                      

                        Time Step
                        
?
                          Integration time step in ps. 0.002 ps is standard with LINCS constraints. Reduce to 0.001 ps for unstable systems.
                        

                      

                      

                        
                      

                    


                    

                      

                        Coord Output
                        
?
                          Output frequency for .xtc trajectory (in steps). 1000 steps × 0.002 ps/step = 2 ps per frame.
                        

                      

                      

                        
                      

                    

                  

                

              


              
              

                

                  

                    Electrostatics & Cutoffs
                    

                      ?
                      Configures coulomb electrostatics computation methods (PME) and cutoff distances.
                    

                  

                  ▼
                

                

                  

                    
                    

                      

                        Coulomb Type
                        
?
                          Electrostatic calculation method. PME (Particle Mesh Ewald) is strongly recommended for accuracy.
                        

                      

                      

                        
                      

                    


                    

                      

                        rcoulomb / rvdw
                        
?
                          Cutoff distance in nm for electrostatic and van der Waals interactions. 1.0 nm is standard.
                        

                      

                      

                        
                        
                      

                    


                    

                      

                        PME Order
                        
?
                          Interpolation order for PME. 4 = cubic (standard). Higher values improve accuracy but increase cost.
                        

                      

                      

                        
                      

                    


                    

                      

                        Fourier Spacing
                        
?
                          Grid spacing in nm for FFT in PME. 0.16 nm is standard. Smaller values increase accuracy and cost.
                        

                      

                      

                        
                      

                    

                  

                

              


              
              

                

                  

                    Algorithms & Constraints
                    

                      ?
                      Configures bond constraints and LINCS algorithm orders to maintain structural stability.
                    

                  

                  ▼
                

                

                  

                    
                    

                      

                        Constraints
                        
?
                          Bond constraint scheme. all-bonds with LINCS is required for dt=0.002 ps. Use h-bonds for dt=0.001 ps.
                        

                      

                      

                        
                      

                    


                    

                      

                        LINCS Order
                        
?
                          Accuracy of LINCS constraint algorithm. 4 is standard. Increase to 6 for highly constrained systems.

                  ?
                  4-character RCSB PDB accession code (e.g. 1AKI, 2KHO). For membrane proteins, prefer pre-oriented structures from the OPM database to ensure correct transmembrane insertion.
                

                
                
              

              

            


          

          

            

              
              

                

                  
                    Upper Leaflet
                    

                      ?
                      Extracellular-facing lipid monolayer. Enter mole fractions (%) for each lipid species; values must sum to 100%. A POPC/POPE/CHL1 mixture is a common starting point.
                    

                  
                  Total: 0%
                

                

                

                  
                

              


              
              

                

                  
                    Lower Leaflet
                    

                      ?
                      Cytoplasmic-facing lipid monolayer. Biological membranes are asymmetric: anionic lipids (POPS, POPE) are enriched in the inner leaflet. Use "Copy Upper" to quickly set a symmetric bilayer.
                    

                  
                  

                    
                    Total: 0%
                  

                

                

                

                  
                

              

            

            


          

          

            

              

                

                  Protein orientation
                  

                    ?
                    Method for orienting the protein in the bilayer. Pre-oriented: uses OPM DB coordinates. Auto-orient: packmol-memgen --orient calculates the tilt angle. Manual: preserves the input coordinates as-is.
                  

                

                

                  
                

              

              

                

                  Protein–membrane distance (nm)
                  

                    ?
                    Minimum distance (nm) between the protein surface and the lipid headgroup plane. 1.0 nm is standard. Too small causes steric clashes; too large inflates the simulation box unnecessarily.
                  

                

                

                  
                

              

              

                

                  Water layer thickness z (nm)
                  

                    ?
                    Thickness of the aqueous slab above and below the bilayer (nm). ≥2.0 nm recommended. An adequate water layer prevents spurious interactions between periodic images of the membrane.
                  

                

                

                  
                

              

              

                

                  Ion concentration (M NaCl)
                  

                    ?
                    NaCl concentration (M) to add to the solvent. Physiological ionic strength is 0.15 M. Counter-ions are also placed to neutralize the net charge of the system.
                  

                

                

                  
                

              

            


          

          

            

              

                

                  Temperature (K)
                  

                    ?
                    Target temperature (K) for the MD simulation. 310 K (37°C) is the physiological reference for membrane simulations. For saturated lipids like DPPC, consider the gel-to-liquid phase transition temperature (~323 K).
                  

                

                

                  
                

              

              

                

                  Simulation time (ns)
                  

                    ?
                    Total simulation length (ns). Membrane equilibration typically requires ≥100 ns. Calculations of lateral diffusion coefficients via MSD analysis may require ≥500 ns for convergence.

                  Net charge
                  

                    ?
                    Formal (net) charge of the ligand. Required for ACPYPE to assign correct AM1-BCC partial charges. Use 0 for neutral molecules; e.g. +1 for protonated amines, −1 for carboxylates.
                  

                

                

                  
                

              

              

                

                  Residue name
                  

                    ?
                    Residue name used in the GROMACS topology and .gro file. Up to 3 alphanumeric characters (e.g. LIG, MOL, INH). Must not clash with standard amino acid residue names (GLY, ALA, etc.).

Counter-ions are also added to neutralize the net charge of the system.
